# JavaScript Developer Role — HR Interview Questions

## About You
1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Walk me through your resume/portfolio.
3. What are your strengths and weaknesses?
4. Why did you choose JavaScript/web development as your career path?
5. What's a project you're most proud of, and what was your specific contribution?

## Motivation & Fit
6. Why do you want to work at this company?
7. What excites you about our product/tech stack?
8. Why are you leaving your current role?
9. What's your expected salary?

## Behavioral
10. Tell me about a time you had a production bug under pressure. How did you handle it?
11. Describe a disagreement with a teammate over a technical approach. How did you resolve it?
12. Tell me about a time you had to learn a new framework/library quickly for a project.
13. How do you handle tight deadlines?
14. Describe a time you received critical feedback on your code. How did you respond?
15. How do you keep your JavaScript skills up to date given how fast the ecosystem changes?

## Logistics
16. What is your notice period?
17. Are you open to relocation/remote/hybrid work?
18. Do you have other offers in hand?
19. Are you comfortable with an on-call/production-support rotation, if applicable?

## Closing
20. Do you have any questions for us? (prepare 2–3: team structure, tech stack, code review culture, growth path)

## Tips
- Keep answers concise and structured (60–90 seconds).
- Tie technical work to business impact (performance improvements, bug reduction, faster delivery).
- Be consistent with what you shared in technical/managerial rounds.
- Show genuine familiarity with the company's product/tech stack.
