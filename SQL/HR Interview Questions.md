# SQL / Database Role — HR Interview Questions

General HR questions, framed for a candidate applying to a SQL-heavy / backend-data role.

## About You

1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Walk me through your resume.
3. What are your strengths and weaknesses? (relate one weakness to something you're actively improving, e.g. "I used to write queries without checking execution plans first — I now make it a habit.")
4. Why do you want to work with us?
5. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

## Motivation & Fit

6. Why did you choose a database/backend-focused role?
7. What do you enjoy most about working with data/databases?
8. What excites you about this company/product?
9. Why are you looking to leave your current role?
10. What's your salary expectation? (research market range beforehand; give a realistic band, not a single fixed number)

## Behavioral

11. Tell me about a time you made a mistake at work and how you handled it. (e.g. an accidental query that affected data — focus on ownership and the fix/prevention, not blame)
12. Tell me about a time you had to learn something new quickly for a project.
13. Describe a conflict with a colleague and how you resolved it.
14. Tell me about a time you had to explain a technical issue to a non-technical stakeholder.
15. How do you handle tight deadlines and pressure?
16. Describe a time you took initiative beyond your assigned tasks.
17. How do you prioritize tasks when everything feels urgent?

## Logistics

18. What is your notice period?
19. Are you open to relocation / remote work / hybrid?
20. Do you have any other offers in hand?
21. Are you comfortable with the on-call/production-support rotation this role may require?

## Closing

22. Do you have any questions for us? (always have 2–3 prepared — e.g. about team structure, tech stack, on-call expectations, growth path)

## Tips
- Keep answers concise (60–90 seconds) unless asked to elaborate.
- Always tie technical strengths back to business impact (e.g. "optimized a query that reduced report load time from 10s to under 1s, improving analyst productivity").
- Be honest and consistent with what you said in technical/managerial rounds.
- Research the company's product and tech stack beforehand — mention it naturally.
