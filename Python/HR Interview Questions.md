# Python Developer Role — HR Interview Questions

## About You
1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Walk me through your resume/portfolio.
3. What are your strengths and weaknesses?
4. Why did you choose Python as your primary language?
5. What's a project you're most proud of, and what was your specific contribution?

## Motivation & Fit
6. Why do you want to join this company?
7. What excites you about our product/tech stack (data, backend, AI, etc.)?
8. Why are you leaving your current role?
9. What's your expected salary?

## Behavioral
10. Tell me about a time you debugged a difficult production issue under pressure.
11. Describe a disagreement with a teammate over a technical/architecture decision. How did you resolve it?
12. Tell me about a time you had to learn a new library/framework quickly for a project.
13. How do you handle tight deadlines?
14. Describe a time you received critical feedback on your code and how you responded.
15. How do you stay current with the fast-changing Python/AI ecosystem?

## Logistics
16. What is your notice period?
17. Are you open to relocation/remote/hybrid work?
18. Do you have other offers in hand?
19. Are you comfortable with an on-call/production-support rotation, if applicable?

## Closing
20. Do you have any questions for us? (prepare 2–3: team structure, tech stack, code review culture, growth path)

## Tips
- Keep answers concise and structured (60–90 seconds).
- Tie technical work to measurable business impact (performance gains, reduced bugs, faster delivery).
- Be consistent with what you shared in technical/managerial rounds.
- Show genuine familiarity with the company's actual product and tech stack.
