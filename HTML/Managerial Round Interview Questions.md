# HTML / Frontend Role — Managerial Round Interview Questions

## Project & Ownership

**Q1. Tell me about a UI project where accessibility or performance was a major requirement. How did you approach it?**
Structure with STAR; mention specific techniques (semantic HTML, lazy loading, WCAG compliance) and measurable outcomes (Lighthouse score improvement, reduced bounce rate).

**Q2. Describe a time you had to fix a critical bug found in production HTML/markup.**
Use a real/realistic example (broken form, SEO regression); focus on diagnosis speed, communication, and the fix.

**Q3. How do you ensure design consistency across a large team of frontend developers?**
Discuss component libraries/design systems, shared HTML/CSS conventions, code review, and style guides.

## Decision-Making & Trade-offs

**Q4. How do you balance pixel-perfect design fidelity against accessibility and performance?**
Discuss negotiating with design on trade-offs (e.g. custom fonts vs load time), and educating stakeholders on why accessibility isn't optional.

**Q5. How do you decide when to build a custom component vs use a native HTML element?**
Prefer native elements for built-in accessibility/behavior; justify custom components only when native ones can't meet a specific UX requirement, and commit to replicating accessibility manually.

**Q6. A stakeholder wants a flashy animation that hurts page performance and accessibility. How do you handle that conversation?**
Discuss presenting data (Core Web Vitals impact, accessibility risk), offering alternatives, and reaching a compromise rather than a flat refusal.

## Team & Process

**Q7. How do you review a teammate's HTML/markup in a PR?**
Check semantic correctness, accessibility (alt text, labels, heading order), responsive behavior, and adherence to team conventions — not just visual correctness.

**Q8. How do you mentor a junior developer who writes non-semantic, div-heavy markup?**
Pair programming, walk through why semantics matter (a11y, SEO, maintainability), share a checklist/style guide, and reinforce with code review feedback.

**Q9. How do you handle disagreement with a designer over an implementation detail?**
Bring concrete constraints/trade-offs (accessibility standards, performance budgets, browser support) rather than opinion; propose alternatives that meet both design intent and technical constraints.

## Tips for Managerial Round
- Use the STAR method for behavioral answers.
- Quantify impact (Lighthouse score, load time, accessibility audit pass rate, conversion rate).
- Show you think about users (accessibility, performance) as much as visuals.
- Be honest about past mistakes and what you changed afterward.
