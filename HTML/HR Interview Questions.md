# HTML / Frontend Role — HR Interview Questions

## About You
1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Walk me through your resume/portfolio.
3. What are your strengths and weaknesses?
4. Why did you choose frontend development?
5. What's a project (from your portfolio) you're most proud of, and why?

## Motivation & Fit
6. Why do you want to join this company?
7. What excites you about our product's user experience?
8. Why are you leaving your current role?
9. What's your expected salary?

## Behavioral
10. Tell me about a time a design was hard to implement in HTML/CSS. How did you handle it?
11. Describe a time you disagreed with a designer or PM. How did you resolve it?
12. Tell me about a time you caught an accessibility or usability issue no one else noticed.
13. How do you handle tight deadlines when a page needs to launch quickly?
14. Describe a time you had to learn a new tool/framework quickly.
15. How do you handle feedback/criticism on your work?

## Logistics
16. What is your notice period?
17. Are you open to relocation/remote/hybrid work?
18. Do you have other offers?

## Closing
19. Do you have any questions for us? (prepare 2–3: team structure, design-to-dev workflow, tech stack, growth opportunities)

## Tips
- Keep answers concise and structured.
- Connect technical work to user/business impact (e.g. "improved page load time, which reduced bounce rate").
- Be consistent with what you said in technical/managerial rounds.
- Show genuine interest in the company's actual product, not generic praise.
